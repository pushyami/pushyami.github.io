Workshops

In my Sophomore and Junior year, I started participating in workshops around the country, in order to gain some perspective on how the work is done outside of my college and to keep myself updated constantly.

Workshop on working of the mind

This workshop was help at IIT-BHU, Varanasi: The holy capital of India. Human mind is a mystery. Distinguished scholars who believe in different schools of thought have met in order to discuss on the working of human mind. It was a mixture of greek thought, modern philosophy and ancient Indian philosophy. This is where I have witnessed and learnt the tarka sastra, a science of dialectics, logic and reasoning, and art of debate that analyzes the nature and source of knowledge and its validity, at its best.

MIT Design Innovation Workshop

I have attended MIT Design Innovation Workshop in January 2015 which introduced me to ideation of a concept and turning it into a prototype. I worked as part of a group of 4. I had complete responsibility for the implementation of the software module, and collaborated with my teammates on design and hardware. It was my first interaction with microcontroller - tessel. Learnt Javascript and node js within 2 days to complete a fun project called bird house, which detects the presence of a bird, records its audio, clicks a picture and remotely transfers the information.

Rethinking Engineering Design Execution (REDX)

As a direct consequence of my work in the Design Innovation Workshop, I was offered an invitation alongside 70 other participants from all over India for the REDX(Rethinking Engineering Design eXecution) workshop conducted by Massachusetts Institute of Technology, (MIT). In this workshop I was given an image processing task of ‘Specular reflection removal in Images’ as a preliminary. In order to complete this task I have spent several sleepless nights during the workshop and completed the task successfully. My own interest and dedication took me by surprise. I learnt a great deal about what I was doing as I was doing it. I got a lot of exposure to a lot of state of the art computing and technical devices, realised a whole new research world exists beyond pure coding and what goes on in IIIT. The most challenging task I faced during this workshop is to pitch the project to a diverse professional audience. Along with specular reflection removal, started with the basic point cloud and basic structure behind 3D reconstruction of objects. My work was well appreciated and I was offered to intern at MIT’s innovation center SRUJANA, in collaboration with LV Prasad’s eye institute to work on the project: Anterior Segment Imaging.
